Understanding Brazilian Investments

For individuals just starting to invest, choosing the right financial product can be overwhelming due to the multitude of available options, each with its own unique characteristics.

This article aims to clarify the investment landscape by covering the following topics:

  • What is an investment?
  • What are the types of financial investments?
  • Types of fixed income investments
  • Types of variable income investments
  • What is the best type of investment?
  • What are the safest types of investments?
  • How to choose a type of investment?

An investment is a financial product designed to generate returns, although this outcome is not guaranteed.
Every investment represents something tangible or intangible, meaning there is always an underlying asset or backing.

For instance, investing in stocks equates to purchasing ownership in a company, while investing in government bonds means buying a portion of the National Treasury's debt.

Investments can be categorized in various ways, including by term or risk modality.
The most common classification separates them into two main types: fixed income and variable income.

Fixed Income Investments

Fixed income investments provide a known return at the time of investment, allowing investors to expect predictable returns, either through a fixed interest rate or a return linked to a specific index.

Variable Income Investments

In contrast, variable income investments do not guarantee predictable returns. The profitability of these investments is influenced by various factors, including the financial health of the underlying company, market conditions, and external economic indicators.

Summary of Investment Types
